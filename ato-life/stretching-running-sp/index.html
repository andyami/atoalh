Mobility — joints & range
1
Neck Rolls
Slow, controlled circles in each direction. Release any tension from sitting or sleeping. Do not force range
30S
2
Arm Circles & Shoulder Openers
Large circles forward then backward. Follow with cross-body arm swings to open the chest. Loosen the upper back
30S
3
Hip Circles
Hands on hips, large circular motion 10 times each direction. Wake up the hip flexors — the most important mobility drill for runners
40S
4
Leg Swings Front to Back
Hold a wall or post. Swing each leg forward and back through full range, 10 per leg. Keep the swinging leg relaxed — let momentum do the work
40S
5
Leg Swings Side to Side
Face the wall, swing each leg side to side across the body 10 times. Opens the adductors and IT band
40S
6
Ankle Circles & Calf Raises
10 circles each foot, then 15 slow calf raises. Primes the calves and Achilles for push-off
45S
Dynamic Activation
7
Walking Lunges with Hip Flexor Hold
10 alternating lunges. At the bottom of each, pause 1 second and press the hip forward. Activates glutes, opens tight hip flexors from desk work
45S
8
High Knees & Butt Kicks
20 high knees on the spot (drive the knee to waist height), then 20 butt kicks (heel to glute). Raises core temp and primes the stride pattern
30S
9
3 × Build Strides — 20m each
Walk back recovery between each. Build from easy jog to race pace over the 20m. The third stride should touch your target session pace
2 MIN
RACE DAY NOTE Add a 1.5km easy jog before the strides. Do the full 9 steps plus jog in the 20 minutes before your start corral. Do not stand still after finishing — keep moving lightly
1
Rest/Nothing
Sitting, standing, walking slowly. No exertion whatsoever.
Rest
2
Very Light
Gentle walk or very slow jog. Breathing barely changes. Could hold this for hours. Feels almost lazy.
Recovery
3
Light — Easy Run
Full sentences with no effort. Breathing is elevated but completely controlled. Legs feel loose, rhythm is automatic. You could talk about your weekend. Target for most training volume.
Easy
4
Moderate-Easy
Full sentences, but you're aware of your breathing. Still genuinely comfortable. The effort is present but effortless to sustain. Legs feel engaged, not strained. Long run pace.
Easy
5
Moderate — Controlled Effort
Phrases, not full paragraphs. Breathing is rhythmic and intentional — you're coordinating it. Legs have some load. This is Chester HM ceiling pace. Should feel sustainable for 90+ minutes.
Race pace
6
Moderate-Hard
Short sentences only — 4 or 5 words before needing a breath. Breathing is working. Sustainable but you're putting in a real shift. Legs are noticeably loading. Used in sharpener sessions.
Threshold
7
Hard
One or two words at a time. Deep, forceful breathing — you can hear yourself working. Legs are heavy. Lactate threshold zone. Maintainable for 20–40 min max.
Threshold
8
Very Hard
Cannot hold a conversation. Breathing is maximal and laboured. Legs burning. This is 5k race effort territory. Not prescribed in your current plan.
Avoid
9
Extremely Hard
At absolute limit of what you can sustain. Gasping. Legs feel like they're failing. Near-maximal output. Not in scope until cardiac clearance received.
Avoid
10
Maximum — All-Out
Cannot be maintained. Sprint finish or absolute max effort. Not achievable for more than a few seconds.
Never

Phrases only, breathing managed? → RPE 4–5
A few words, working hard? → RPE 6–7
Cannot speak, breathing maximal? → RPE 8–10
